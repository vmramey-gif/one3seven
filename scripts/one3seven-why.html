The core insight

Most intake platforms optimize for the firm. Most legal aid tools optimize for the worker. one3Seven is built on the belief that if the worker's story is presented well, the firm makes a better decision — and both sides win. The intake packet a firm sees is only as good as what the worker was able to communicate. one3Seven bridges that gap.

How It Works
From worker submission to firm decision — end to end
Step 1 — Worker
Worker submits their intake
Guided question flow captures employment dates, key people, what happened, and when. The worker uploads their documents — pay stubs, termination letters, HR emails — directly into the platform. No phone tag. No intake form that gets lost in email.
Step 2 — Platform
one3Seven builds the intake packet
AI extracts the timeline of events, identifies key quotes from uploaded documents, surfaces timing gaps (complaint → warning → termination), flags confirmed facts, and generates a structured intelligence layer for the reviewing attorney. Every packet is the same clean format.
Step 3 — Firm
Attorney reviews the pre-built packet
The firm sees a structured intake card: overview, timeline, AI intelligence, uploaded files, confirmed facts, and what's still missing. Review takes 10–20 minutes — not 3–6 hours. The attorney has everything needed to make a confident decision.
Step 4 — Decision
One-click outcome with worker visibility
The firm accepts the case, requests additional documents, or marks it as not pursuing — all tracked in the platform. The worker isn't left wondering. The firm has a documented record. The loop closes cleanly.

What makes the AI layer different

one3Seven doesn't just store documents — it reads them. The platform identifies retaliation timing intervals (e.g., "termination 34 days after HR complaint"), direct quotes from uploaded files that are legally significant, and what's missing from the current record set. The attorney sees not just what the worker submitted — but what it means.

The one rule

one3Seven does not tell firms which cases to take. It does not give workers legal advice. It does one thing: it makes sure the right information reaches the right attorney in the clearest possible format. What happens after that is between the firm and the worker — as it should be.
