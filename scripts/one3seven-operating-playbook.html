Why This Document Exists

Every company eventually reaches a moment where the founding idea is understood only by the people who built it. Everyone else is guessing — about the mission, the product, what to say, what not to say, what to build next, and why certain decisions are non-negotiable.

one3Seven reached that moment earlier than most, because the product operates in a space where imprecise language creates real risk. Employment law is sensitive. Worker records are sensitive. Attorney relationships are governed by professional rules. What we say — and what we don't say — matters.

This playbook exists so that every person who touches one3Seven — whether they write code, talk to firms, advise on strategy, or run a pilot — understands the product correctly before they act on it.

It is not a marketing document. It is not a vision deck. It is a working manual for doing this right.

The core thing you need to understand is this:

Core Statement

one3Seven exists to organize a worker's story and records before attorney review, so firms can begin with a clearer chronology, source documents, and open questions. The attorney makes every decision. The platform never does.

Everything in this document flows from that. If a feature, a message, or a decision conflicts with it, the decision rule is simple: don't do it.

Study this. Refer back to it. Update it when the product changes. But don't change the mission.

Part 1
The Founding Idea
The problem we saw, why it matters, and what we decided to do about it.

The Problem We Saw

When a worker believes they have been wronged at work — underpaid, retaliated against, unfairly terminated — they face an immediate and practical problem. They have a story, but their story is scattered.

It lives in text messages, email threads, pay stubs, an offer letter they printed in 2021, a termination letter they photographed with their phone, and memory. None of it is organized. None of it is dated in a useful way. None of it has been read by anyone who could tell them whether it matters.

At the same time, an employment attorney is sitting across the city waiting for exactly this kind of intake — but what arrives is either a long, emotional phone call with no documents, or a pile of files with no coherent story attached to them.

Why Intake Is the Real Bottleneck

Before an attorney can evaluate a matter, someone has to do work that doesn't require an attorney. Someone has to:

  • Build a timeline of what happened and when
  • Identify what documents exist and what is missing
  • Connect events to the records that support them
  • Separate what the worker believes happened from what the documents actually show
  • Ask the right follow-up questions to fill the gaps

In most employment law practices, this work falls to the attorney — during the consultation, or before it, or never. It is expensive, slow, and often incomplete. Matters that might have been evaluated are abandoned at the intake stage because nobody had time to assemble them.

Why Existing Solutions Don't Solve It

Intake forms collect data fields. Document upload tools store files. Case management platforms track what happened after representation began. None of them bridge the specific gap between a worker's raw story and the structured review that an attorney actually needs.

The Founding Insight

The intake bottleneck is not about access to attorneys. It is about disorganized information arriving before the first conversation. one3Seven performs the initial organization pass — so firms can begin evaluation with a structured record instead of a pile of files.

What We Decided to Build

A platform that guides workers through telling their story, helps them upload the records that support it, performs a deterministic organization of those records into a structured intake, and delivers a source-linked, chronological review record to an employment law firm — before the first consultation.

Not legal advice. Not a law firm. Not a case-value predictor. An intake organization and review platform.

Part 2
What one3Seven Is
The precise definition — and what it is not. Mandatory reading for everyone.
Product Definition

one3Seven is an employment-intake organization and review platform that turns worker-submitted stories and records into structured, source-linked intake information for independent attorney review.

What It Is

  • A guided intake tool that helps workers document their employment story
  • A document upload and organization platform for employment records
  • A structured review interface for employment law firms
  • A source-tracing system that links events and excerpts to the original files
  • A chronology builder that sequences documented events by date
  • A gap-detection tool that identifies missing records before consultation
  • A document-request channel between firms and workers
  • A firm-controlled intake workflow: review, request, accept, or decline

What It Is Not

Non-Negotiable Definitions — Do Not Misrepresent

These must be understood and respected by every person who represents one3Seven in any context.

❌ Not a Law Firm

  • No attorney-client relationship is formed through the platform
  • No licensed attorney supervises one3Seven's operations
  • Representation requires a separate written agreement

❌ Not Legal Advice

  • No claim assessment, evaluation, or opinion is provided
  • Workers are not told whether they have a case
  • Attorneys are not told what a case is worth

❌ Not a Claim Scorer

  • No strength score, confidence level, or case grade
  • No prediction of legal outcomes
  • No assessment of the likelihood of representation

❌ Not a Referral Marketplace

  • Workers are not matched to the "right" firm
  • No referral fees or placement commissions
  • Firm participation does not imply endorsement

❌ Not a Guarantee

  • No guarantee of review, response, or representation
  • No guarantee of filing deadline accuracy
  • No guarantee of organized output completeness

❌ Not AI-Decided

  • Every firm decision is made by a human attorney
  • Extracted facts require attorney review and confirmation
  • The platform organizes — it does not conclude
Part 3
The People We Serve
Workers and firms have different needs. The platform is built around both.

Workers

Workers who believe they have been wronged at work. They may be dealing with wage theft, unpaid overtime, retaliation after a complaint, wrongful termination, or discrimination. They are often anxious, unsure of what they have, and unsure whether what they have is enough.

Their Needs

  • Help telling their story clearly and completely
  • Help identifying which records matter
  • Clarity about what was submitted and what is missing
  • Control over who sees their information
  • Simple, plain language throughout the process
  • Transparency about where their intake stands
  • Confidence that their documents are not shared without consent
  • A way to respond to firm requests for additional records

What They Are Not

  • Not clients until represented in writing
  • Not guaranteed a review or response from any firm
  • Not told whether they have a legal claim

Firms

Employment law firms and solo practitioners who receive and evaluate potential client intakes. They range from high-volume practices to boutique firms. Their constraint is time — specifically, the time it takes to move from first contact to a decision about whether to pursue.

Their Needs

  • Faster initial review without sacrificing accuracy
  • Chronology before the first consultation
  • Direct access to source documents
  • Clear signals about what is missing
  • Fewer back-and-forth collection steps
  • A structured way to request additional records
  • Confidence that the platform doesn't make legal decisions
  • A workflow that fits their existing intake process

What They Are Not

  • Not obligated to review, respond to, or accept any intake
  • Not verified as to their practice quality or outcomes
  • Not endorsed by one3Seven as the "right" firm for any worker

"The platform serves both sides by doing one thing for each. For workers: it organizes. For firms: it structures the review. It does not decide anything for either party."

Part 4
The Core Product Journey
Both workflows, step by step. This is what the product actually does.

Worker Journey

1
Arrives via firm link or direct sign-up
Worker enters through a firm-specific intake URL or discovers the platform independently. Firm code, if present, is stored and linked to the intake automatically.
2
Creates an account
Email/password or Google sign-in. Role is set to worker. Contact information collected.
3
Tells their story
Guided 8-question intake covering employment context, key people, timeline, and what happened. Answers are stored as the worker's own narrative — not transformed or summarized.
4
Tags matter type
Selects from: wage theft, overtime, retaliation, wrongful termination, discrimination, harassment, FMLA, misclassification. These are organizational tags — not legal determinations.
5
Uploads records
Drag-and-drop upload of PDFs, documents, and text files. System confirms upload, infers document category from filename and content, and stores in secure intake storage.
6
Records are organized
The deterministic pipeline extracts text, dates, and key information. A source-linked timeline is built. Missing record types are flagged. A structured summary is assembled.
7
Reviews intake summary and controls sharing
Worker sees the organized timeline and document list. Chooses to route to a firm by code or participating network. Consent is recorded at time of sharing.
8
Responds to firm requests and tracks status
Worker receives requests for additional documents, uploads responses, and sees plain-language status updates at each stage of the review process.

Firm Journey

1
Account created and reviewed
Firm creates an account during beta. Accounts are manually reviewed before intakes are routed. Firm code is generated for direct intake links.
2
Receives intake in review queue
Dashboard shows all routed intakes with status: new, preview, full access, accepted, or not pursuing. Notifications alert firm to new arrivals.
3
Opens intake review
Firm sees: worker context (clearly labeled as worker-provided, not verified), organized chronology, source-linked intelligence, document categories, readiness indicators, and missing-record flags.
4
Reviews original files
Full-access routes allow direct file viewing with signed URLs. Source document review is the final authority — the platform's organization is the starting point.
5
Requests additional documents if needed
Firm selects document categories and adds a note. Worker receives the request and uploads a response. Firm is notified when documents arrive.
6
Makes intake decision
Firm accepts (continues toward representation), declines (not pursuing), or continues review. All decisions are firm-controlled. one3Seven records the outcome for measurement.
Part 5
The Mind Layer
What the intelligence system does — and exactly what it does not do. The most important technical section.
Core Philosophy

The mind layer organizes and surfaces. It does not analyze, conclude, or advise. Every output is traceable to a source document or a worker statement. Nothing is invented.

What the Intelligence Layer Does

  • Extracts text from uploaded records (PDF text layer, TXT, HTML)
  • Categorizes documents by type using filename and content signals
  • Extracts dates, employer names, and key people from document text
  • Builds a dated chronology of documented events
  • Links each chronology event to its source file(s)
  • Separates worker statements from document-derived information
  • Surfaces relevant excerpts and key document language
  • Identifies timing relationships between documented events
  • Flags missing or common-but-absent record types
  • Creates structured summaries for attorney review
  • Identifies dates that may warrant timely attorney attention
  • Extracts structured facts per document category (complaint topic, stated reason, warning date, pay figures)
  • Synthesizes per-document facts into an intake-level intelligence view
  • Labels all worker-provided content as worker-provided
  • Tracks source strength per event (strong / partial / inferred / needs review)

What the Intelligence Layer Does Not Do

Firm and Team Reminder — These Capabilities Do Not Exist

Do not imply, demonstrate, or promise any of the following.

  • Determine whether a legal claim exists
  • Score claim strength or legal merit
  • Decide who is telling the truth
  • Confirm that every extracted fact is accurate
  • Determine whether retaliation occurred
  • Calculate a guaranteed filing deadline
  • Identify the applicable agency or jurisdiction
  • Predict case outcomes
  • Replace original document review
  • Replace attorney judgment
  • Process scanned image files (OCR not currently implemented)
  • Detect contradictions between documents
  • Verify employer identity or HR authenticity
  • Guarantee output completeness for any intake

How Output Quality Varies

Intelligence output depends on what the worker submitted. A complete record set — offer letter, pay stubs, HR correspondence, warning, termination letter — produces a richer output. An intake with only a termination letter produces a thinner output. Both are correct behavior. The platform is not failing when output is sparse; the records are sparse.

Attorneys must always treat platform output as a starting point for review, not a finished analysis.

The Two Processing Paths

Phase 2A — Deterministic Pipeline

  • Runs on every intake automatically
  • PDF text extraction, filename-based classification
  • Rule-based timeline construction
  • Template-phrase summary generation
  • Banned-output guardrails prevent legal-conclusion language
  • Always produces some output regardless of document quality

Phase 2B — Intelligence Extraction

  • Runs when a full-access firm opens an intake
  • Claude API extracts structured facts per document
  • Synthesis produces timing intervals, key quotes, facts
  • Output depends on document text quality
  • Non-blocking: failure does not affect base intake review
  • Confirmed operational as of June 2026

Sales Playbook

The Opening Frame

Opening Statement

Firms often receive stories, screenshots, pay records, complaints, and termination documents in different places — and someone has to organize them before the attorney can begin. one3Seven performs that initial organization pass automatically, so the intake arrives structured with a chronology and source documents already attached.

Discovery Questions

Before demonstrating, understand how the firm works:

  • How does your firm currently collect employment records from prospective clients?
  • Who does the work of organizing documents before an attorney reviews them?
  • How many follow-up calls or emails are typically needed to get a complete picture?
  • What information is most often missing when an intake arrives?
  • How do attorneys currently see the sequence of events in a matter?
  • How quickly can the firm decide whether to continue reviewing an intake?
  • What causes potentially useful intakes to stall or be abandoned?

Part 10
Product Decision Rules
How to decide what to build, defer, and reject. Use this before every feature discussion.

The Seven Questions

Every proposed feature, change, or addition must answer all seven:

  1. Does this improve worker intake quality, clarity, or ease?
  2. Does this improve firm review usefulness or efficiency?
  3. Does this strengthen the mind layer — organization, source tracing, or intelligence?
  4. Does this reduce friction in the core worker-to-firm loop?
  5. Has a real user asked for it more than once, or will a real user need it for the pilot?
  6. Does it create legal, privacy, or operational risk that outweighs the benefit?
  7. Can it wait until after the first pilot produces evidence?

If the answer to question 7 is yes, defer it. If the answer to question 6 is yes, stop and review before proceeding.

Decision Categories

Build Now
Directly unblocks the pilot or fixes a confirmed defect
Test Manually
Needs real-world validation before building infrastructure
Pilot-Only
Useful but needs firm feedback to confirm value before full build
Defer
Good idea but no evidence it is needed now. Revisit after pilot.
Reject
Conflicts with product vision, creates unacceptable risk, or belongs to a different product

The Governing Rule

"Hardening, not building. One prompt, one defect, one expected behavior change. No opportunistic cleanup. No scope expansion during the pilot stabilization sprint."

Current Sprint Priority Order

  1. RLS verification — real-data security gate, non-negotiable
  2. Pending-extraction smoke test — validation only, no edits unless it fails
  3. Interrupted organization recovery — genuine pilot-blocking bug
  4. Voice input — hide immediately if persistence cannot be proven
  5. Minimum firm email notifications — one channel, two events
  6. Event-timing investigation — improvement, not a blocker
Part 11
Safety and Trust Rules
Non-negotiable operational rules. These are not guidelines. They are boundaries.

Data and Privacy Rules

  • No real worker data may be used before security gates (RLS verification, organization recovery, consent flow) are confirmed complete
  • No production RLS policy may be modified without a full read-only audit first
  • No private information — worker narrative, allegations, employment details, document excerpts — may be included in notification emails
  • No client-side secrets. API keys, service role credentials, and environment variables belong server-side only
  • No cross-account access. A firm may only see intakes routed to them by their firm ID
  • No preview firm may access documents available only to full-access routes

Product Integrity Rules

  • No AI output without source attribution where available
  • No silent failures that leave workers believing information was saved when it was not
  • No demo feature represented as currently operational unless it works for real intakes
  • No legal conclusions, claim-strength language, deadline guarantees, or outcome predictions in any user-facing copy
  • No "verified" label applied to information that has not been independently confirmed
  • No attorney-client relationship implied through platform language

Engineering Rules

  • No broad refactors during the pilot stabilization sprint
  • No schema or migration changes without confirming RLS impact
  • No dependency installations without explicit approval
  • No deployments without a passing build, a pre-deploy phrase sweep, and a smoke test
  • No code changes to the intelligence pipeline, source trace codec, or banned-output logic without a full investigation pass first
  • Email failure must never roll back or block the intake workflow
Absolute Boundary

The platform organizes. It does not advise, conclude, guarantee, or represent. When any person, feature, or message crosses that line — stop and correct it before moving forward.

Technical Operating Rules
The engineering standard for every change to one3Seven during the pilot period.

The Engineering Workflow

Every change — no matter how small — follows this sequence:

1
Audit first
Read the relevant files. Understand current behavior before forming a hypothesis.
2
Identify root cause
Confirm the exact failing boundary with evidence. Do not assume.
3
Checkpoint
Create a clean backup copy before any changes. Document the current state.
4
Minimal patch
Change only what is required. No opportunistic cleanup. No related improvements.
5
Build
Run npm run build. Zero new errors required. Pre-existing warnings are documented.
6
Targeted tests
Run tests covering the changed behavior. Document any pre-existing failures.
7
Smoke test
Manually verify the specific expected behavior change. Confirm no protected behavior changed.
8
Deploy
Deploy from a clean timestamped copy via vercel.cmd --prod. Confirm production alias.
9
Stop
Do not begin the next fix in the same session. Close the session after verification.

Do-Not-Touch Systems
