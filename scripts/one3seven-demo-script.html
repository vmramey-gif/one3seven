Before the Call

Send the link ahead of time

Send the demo link before the call. Tell them to hold off opening it until you're on together. This way you walk through it side by side — they're not confused and clicking ahead.

"I'm sending you a link — hold off on opening it until we're on together."
Open The first thing you say 20 sec
"What you're looking at is exactly what an attorney at your firm would see when a case gets routed to you through one3Seven. This is a real worker's case file — built entirely by the platform. The worker uploaded their documents, told their story, and this is what came out the other side. No intake coordinator touched it."
Why: You're not pitching features. You're placing them inside the product immediately. They're the attorney. This is their screen.
Stop 1 The slim header bar 30 sec
Point to →
Top bar — "one3Seven · Firm intake review" + "Sample intake" pill
"In the live product this bar shows your firm name and the intake number. The 'Sample intake' tag is just telling you this is a demo record — in production it would be a live case. Everything below this line is identical to what your team would actually see."
"And that strip at the bottom — that's just a 'Get Started' prompt for demo visitors. Your attorneys won't see that."
Why: Removes the "is this real?" skepticism before it forms.
Stop 2 The case snapshot card 1 min
Point to →
First card — employer name, employment dates, key people
"This is the first thing your attorney sees — the shape of the case. Worker's name, employer, how long they worked there, who the key people are. This came from the documents, not from a phone call."
"In this case — Pacific Ridge Distribution LLC, warehouse associate, two years and eight months. The key people are already named: the shift supervisor, the HR manager. Your attorney walks into the first consultation already knowing who they're dealing with."
Why: Specific names and dates make it feel like a real case. That's when they start leaning in.

The Core Walkthrough
Stop 3 Firm Actions card 45 sec
Point to →
Accept · Not Pursuing buttons + Download Intake Summary
"These are the three decisions your firm makes. Accept it and it goes into your follow-up queue. Pass on it — and it stays in your system for reference, the worker isn't notified, it's internal only. Or download the full intake packet as a PDF to bring into your case management system."
"One screen. One decision. That's the whole intake workflow from the firm's side."
Why: Shows the closed loop — the firm isn't just reading, they're acting. Decision fatigue is real in intake; this makes it simple.
The Moment The timeline — this is where it lands 2.5 min
Point to →
Timeline events — scroll slowly through each one
"This is the timeline the platform reconstructed from the documents. Not from what the worker said — from what the records actually show."

Read these events out loud as you scroll:

Q4 2022 – Q1 2023
Overtime hours recorded without matching pay rate
March – July 2023
Missed meal-break entries across 14 documented shifts
August 7, 2024
Formal HR complaint filed — payroll audit requested
August 18, 2024 — 11 days later
Written warning — "attitude and conduct." No prior record.
October 11, 2024 — 34 days after complaint
Terminated — "performance deficiency." No PIP. No prior warnings.
Stop here. Let them sit with it.
"That sequence — complaint, warning eleven days later, termination thirty-four days after — that's the retaliation timeline. The platform surfaced it automatically. Before one3Seven, an intake coordinator pieces that together from a stack of PDFs. That's an hour of work, minimum. Here it's on the screen before your attorney says a word."
Why this is the moment: The timing gap between complaint and termination is the kind of thing that takes hours to find manually. They will feel this. Don't rush past it.

The Close
Stop 5 Verified Facts card 1 min
Point to →
Green "Verified Facts — From document content" card
"This section is different from the rest. Everything above it is organized from the worker's narrative. This is pulled directly from the text of the documents themselves."
"The complaint topic, the HR response, the warning language, the termination reason — all sourced from the actual files. And at the bottom: key document quotes. Verbatim language from the termination letter and the written warning, pulled automatically. Your attorney walks into the first call with this already in hand. That's a different kind of conversation."
Why: Separates one3Seven from "AI that summarizes things." This is AI that extracts and cites. That's what attorneys can actually trust.
Stop 6 Documents section 45 sec
Point to →
Document categories and file list
"Eleven documents across five categories — payroll records, time records, HR communications, termination records, handbook excerpt. The worker uploaded these. The platform categorized them, named them, and made them accessible at the right access level."
"No shared Dropbox. No email chain. No 'can you resend that file.' It's all here, organized, when the case arrives."
Stop 7 Clarifications Needed 30 sec
Point to →
Amber "Items Requiring Confirmation" card
"This is what's still missing. Prior performance reviews aren't in the file yet — which matters for the wrongful termination argument. Coworker corroboration isn't documented. The platform isn't saying the case is weak. It's telling your attorney exactly what to ask for in the first call."
Why: This card shows the platform thinks like a lawyer — proactively flagging gaps, not just organizing what exists.
Close Stop scrolling. Look at them. Say this. 1 min
"What you just saw represents two to four hours of intake work — eliminated. Before your attorney has said a word to this worker, they already know the employer, the timeline, the key documents, the retaliation sequence, and what's still missing."
"Most firms screen cases they never convert. They spend intake hours on cases that go nowhere. one3Seven doesn't change whether a case is good or bad — it makes the screening fast enough that passing on a case costs you five minutes instead of five hours."
"The question for you is: how does this fit into what your firm is already doing?"
Stop. Let them respond. Do not fill the silence.

Objection Responses
They say You say
"How accurate is the AI?" The Verified Facts card cites every claim back to the source document — file name, category, verbatim quote. Attorneys verify, they don't trust blindly. It's a starting point, not a conclusion.
"What about confidentiality?" Workers control access. A firm only sees a limited preview until the worker explicitly approves full access. Nothing is shared without consent. The worker can see exactly what the firm sees.
"We have our own intake process." We're not replacing it — we're delivering to it. Think of this as the packet that arrives before your process starts, already organized. Your intake coordinator starts from a finished document instead of a blank screen.
"What does it cost?" That's the conversation for a follow-up. Today I wanted you to see what the product does. Can we get 30 minutes on the calendar this week?
"Is the data secure?" Everything is encrypted in transit and at rest, access-controlled at the database level, and the firm never holds worker data directly — they're reading through the platform, not downloading a raw file.
"What happens to the worker?" The worker gets a live status tracker showing exactly where their case is, and gets notified when a firm takes action. They're not in the dark — they know the moment a firm reviews their file.
"Do you work with our case management system?" Integration is on the roadmap. Right now, the download button exports a full intake packet as a PDF that drops into any system you're already using. It's format-agnostic.
"Is this HIPAA / bar compliant?" We're building the compliance documentation now and happy to walk through it specifically. The data model is designed around lawyer-client privilege principles from day one — happy to have that conversation with your IT or compliance team.

The One Rule

Never say "we're working on that" or "that's coming soon."

If something isn't built, say: "That's a great question for the follow-up — I want to make sure I give you the right answer." Then move on.

You are selling the thing that exists today, not the roadmap. The demo already does enough. Let it speak.
