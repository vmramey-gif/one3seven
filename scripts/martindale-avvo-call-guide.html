Know This First

Who Martindale-Avvo Is

Martindale-Avvo is the largest legal lead generation company in the country. They own both Martindale-Hubbell (attorney directory, ratings) and Avvo (consumer-facing attorney marketplace). Attorneys pay them for directory listings, leads, and intake traffic.

Their Core Business Problem

Martindale-Avvo sends attorneys leads — but leads are just contact information and a few sentences. The handoff from "person submitted a form" to "attorney actually takes the case" is full of friction, drop-off, and wasted time for both sides. They know this better than anyone. That gap is exactly what one3Seven addresses. That's the conversation.

Why This Contact Matters

An associate at Martindale-Avvo isn't a decision-maker — but they're an insider who understands the lead quality problem from the inside. The goal is to learn how they see the space and plant a seed. If this person sees the fit, they can open the right door internally.

The Opening

How to Start the Call

Lead with the relationship, then the reason for the call. Keep it honest and low-pressure.

Opening Line — Say This

"Hey — I know you're deep in this space so I'll keep it short. We're building something in the employment law intake world that I think is complementary to what Martindale-Avvo does, not competitive. I'm not pitching you today. I just want to understand how you're thinking about the lead quality problem and share what problem we're solving. If there's something worth exploring, we'll know pretty quickly."

Why This Opening Works

It's honest. It removes pressure. It positions the conversation as peer-to-peer between people who understand the industry. And it signals you respect their time — which earns you more of it.

The Conversation

What to Talk About

These are your three talking points. Each one is designed to invite them to react — not to explain the product.

  1. Lead quality, not lead volume

    "The employment firms we talk to aren't asking for more leads. They're asking for better-organized leads. The intake handoff is broken — workers submit contact forms, firms call back days later, half the information is missing. We're focused on that gap." Then stop. Let them react. They know this problem. Let them say it back to you.

  2. The worker-first angle

    "Most lead gen is built for the firm — it's about getting attorneys in front of clients. We came at it from the other direction: what does a worker actually need to get their situation in front of an attorney in a way that gets taken seriously? That reframe leads somewhere different." This is interesting enough to create curiosity without revealing what the product actually does.

  3. The niche focus

    "We're starting in plaintiff employment — retaliation, wrongful termination, wage-and-hour. It's a segment with high volume, real document complexity, and firms that are selective about what they take. That selectivity creates an interesting intake problem." Shows domain knowledge and focus without showing the product.

The Power Move

The One Question to Ask

Ask This — Then Listen

"From where you sit — what's the biggest drop-off point between a worker submitting a lead and a firm actually engaging them?"

Why This Question Wins the Call

Their answer tells you everything: whether they see the same problem you're solving, how sophisticated their thinking is, and whether there's a natural story about how one3Seven fits alongside their lead flow. It also makes them feel heard — you're asking for their expertise, not just selling at them. Take notes. Their answer is market research.

The Close

How to End the Call

Closing Line — Say This

"I'd love to get our founders on a call with you once we're a little further along — just to show you what we've built and get your honest reaction. You know this space better than almost anyone. Your feedback would actually be useful to us, not just a sales conversation."

Why This Close Works

You're asking for their expertise, not their business. That framing keeps the relationship warm, removes the pressure of a sales ask, and makes the second call about them — which means they're more likely to say yes.

If They Ask What You're Building

The One-Liner (And Nothing More)

If they push for a product description before you're ready to share details, use exactly this:

Product Description — One Sentence Only

"We help employment workers organize and submit their records so attorneys receive a structured intake instead of a phone call. Think of it as the layer between the lead and the consultation."

That's accurate, it's interesting, and it doesn't give away anything proprietary. If they want more — that's the second call. Say: "I'd rather show you than describe it — can we get 30 minutes on the calendar?"

Full Reference

What to Say vs. What to Never Say

✓ Say This
  • "The intake handoff is broken — we're focused on that gap"
  • "We came at it from the worker's side, not the firm's side"
  • "Complementary to what you do, not competitive"
  • "Starting in plaintiff employment — retaliation, wage-and-hour"
  • "Firms receive a structured intake instead of a phone call"
  • "The layer between the lead and the consultation"
  • "I want to understand how you see the space"
  • "Your feedback would be useful to us"
  • "We're a little further along — can we get 30 minutes?"
✗ Never Say This
  • "We use AI / Claude / Anthropic to read documents"
  • "We extract facts from PDFs automatically"
  • "We built a Verified Facts feature"
  • Anything about the tech stack or infrastructure
  • "We're building a competitor to your intake product"
  • Your pricing model or revenue structure
  • Specific firm clients or firm names
  • Worker names or any intake data
  • "We score or rank cases for firms"
  • "The AI tells attorneys what cases to take"
  • Funding status or runway
  • How many intakes are live in the system
Hard Stops

Topics to Avoid Entirely on This Call

These topics either reveal too much, create the wrong impression, or open conversations you're not ready to have yet.

🚫 Do Not Discuss: The AI Layer

The AI extraction feature is one3Seven's most defensible technology. Describing it in a first call — before NDAs or trust are established — hands a well-resourced competitor a roadmap. The phrase "AI-powered intake" is fine as concept. The mechanics are off-limits.

🚫 Do Not Discuss: Partnership Structure or Revenue Share

Martindale-Avvo will immediately want to know "what's in it for us." If you answer that question on the first call, you've started a negotiation you're not prepared for. Keep it at: "That's a conversation for when we're both clear on whether there's a fit."

🚫 Do Not Discuss: Current Client or Firm List

Even if it would impress them — the firms currently using the platform are confidential. Namedropping creates liability and violates the trust those firms placed in you.

🚫 Do Not Discuss: Exclusivity or White-Label

They may fish for this — "could we be your exclusive employment law partner?" or "could this be Avvo-branded?" Don't engage with that yet. The answer is: "That's worth a real conversation — let's get the right people in the room first."

🚫 Do Not Discuss: Weaknesses or What's Not Built Yet

No integrations, no volume stats, no "we're still figuring out X." First calls are about fit and curiosity. Save the honest warts-and-all conversation for when there's mutual interest and an NDA.

Reading the Room

How to Read the Call

🟢 It's Going Well If...

They're asking questions about how it works. They're telling you about their own lead quality problem. They mention specific attorneys or firm types. They say "that's interesting" or "I hadn't thought about it that way." They ask who else is in the space.


Do: Keep listening. Ask follow-up questions. End by booking the second call before you hang up.

🟡 Pump the Brakes If...

They start asking about pricing, volume guarantees, or tech details. They mention their own intake product. They ask if you're raising money. They want to loop in their boss immediately.


Do: Slow down. "That's a great question for a second conversation — I want to make sure the right people are in the room." Get off the call cleanly.

If They Push Back

Quick Objection Responses

❓ "We already have an intake product — why would we need this?"
"That's exactly the conversation I want to have. Your intake product captures the lead — ours is what happens after. If there's a fit, it's additive. I'd rather show you and let you decide."
❓ "How is this different from what we already do?"
"You connect workers to attorneys. We organize what the worker brings so the attorney can actually evaluate it. Different problem, different layer — which is why I used the word complementary."
❓ "Can you just send me a deck?"
"I'll send you something, but honestly a 20-minute call with our founder is more useful than a deck at this stage — the product is easier to see than describe. Can we find 20 minutes in the next two weeks?"
❓ "We'd want exclusivity in the legal space."
"That's a real conversation worth having — with the right people in the room and some mutual understanding of what we're each bringing. Let's get to that second call first."
