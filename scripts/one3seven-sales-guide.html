The One-Sentence Pitch

Before anything else, memorize this:

The Pitch

one3Seven helps employment workers organize and submit their records to participating attorneys — so firms receive a structured, document-backed intake packet instead of a phone call or web form.

That's it. You're not selling AI. You're not selling lead gen. You're selling a better first handoff between a potential client and their attorney.

Section 2

Two Users, One Platform

one3Seven has two completely separate sides. You need to understand both.

👷

The Worker

A current or former employee who believes their rights were violated — wrongful termination, wage theft, harassment, retaliation. They use one3Seven to tell their story and upload their documents.


What they get: A guided intake experience. A way to organize what happened. A connection to a participating firm.

⚖️

The Firm

A plaintiff employment law firm — solo practitioners to mid-size boutiques. They use one3Seven to review incoming intakes, access organized documents, and decide whether to take a case.


What they get: A structured intake packet with a timeline, key documents, and attorney questions pre-built.

Important: Workers Do Not Pay

Workers use one3Seven for free. The product is entirely on the firm side. Firms pay for access to intake packets and the review infrastructure.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

Step 1 — Worker tells their story

The worker fills out a guided intake form. They describe what happened, who was involved, key dates, and whether they were paid correctly. The platform asks smart follow-up questions (Did you file an HR complaint? Were you given a reason for termination?). This is not a blank form — it's a structured conversation.

Step 2 — Worker uploads documents

The worker uploads whatever they have: paystubs, offer letters, termination letters, text messages, HR emails, written warnings, meal break logs, coworker statements. They don't need everything — even 3–4 documents produce a useful packet. The platform categorizes each file automatically.

Step 3 — Platform organizes everything

This is where one3Seven does the work. The platform:

  • Builds a chronological timeline from the documents
  • Links each event to its source document
  • Reads the PDFs with AI to extract key facts (dates, stated reasons, key quotes)
  • Generates 8 priority questions an attorney should ask
  • Flags items that need confirmation (date discrepancies, post-termination statements)
  • Notes what documents are missing and would strengthen the record

Step 4 — Firm reviews the packet

The firm sees a live intake review screen with the timeline, supporting documents, and structured facts. They can also download a formatted PDF packet to share internally or save to their matter management system.

Step 5 — Firm decides

Inside the platform, the firm can: accept the intake, request additional documents, request clarification from the worker, or decline. Their decision is tracked.

What's In the Intake Packet

This is the product's core deliverable. Firms get a 10-section review packet — live on screen and downloadable as a PDF. Know these sections cold.

# Section Name What It Contains Why Firms Care
1 Review Snapshot Worker name, employer, dates, document count, summary of what records exist Answers "is this worth opening?" in 10 seconds
2 Why This Requires Review Auto-generated narrative: the sequence of events from the documents Tells the story without the firm having to piece it together

10 Firm Review Options Available actions, missing document suggestions, confirmation item count Clear next steps — no ambiguity about what to do after reviewing
The AI Layer (Verified Facts)

After a firm gains full access to an intake, the platform automatically reads each uploaded PDF with AI. It extracts: stated termination reason, complaint topic, overtime flags, missed break patterns, key quotes from documents, and timing between events. These appear as Verified Facts — clearly labeled as document-extracted, never as legal conclusions.

Section 5

Access Levels — How Firms See Intakes

Not every firm sees every intake the same way. There are two states:

👁️

Preview Access

Firm sees basic facts: employer, approximate dates, document categories, event count. No actual documents. Enough to decide if they want full access.


Typical flow: Worker submits → firm gets preview → firm requests full access → worker approves.

🔓

Full Access

Firm sees everything: the complete packet, all source documents (downloadable), verified facts from AI extraction, and all timeline details.


Requires: Worker approval OR firm-code intake (firm sent the worker directly).

Firm-Code Intakes

A firm can generate a unique intake link to send directly to a potential client. When a worker comes through that link, the firm automatically gets full access — no approval step needed. This is how firms use one3Seven as their own intake infrastructure.

Section 8

Handling Objections

These are the five questions every firm asks. Know the answers cold.

❓ "We already have an intake process. Why would we change it?"
You don't have to change it. one3Seven adds a structured document layer before the first consultation — your intake staff still does what they do. The question is whether you want to walk into every consultation already knowing what documents exist, what the termination letter says, and what the retaliation timeline looks like.
❓ "We're worried about AI making legal conclusions."
one3Seven never makes legal conclusions. It extracts facts from documents — dates, stated reasons, quotes — and flags what requires attorney review. It explicitly says "this is not a legal analysis, theory of the case, or outcome prediction" in every packet. Your attorneys make every decision. The platform just organizes the record before they do.
❓ "Does this integrate with Clio / Lawmatics / our system?"
Right now, every intake is downloadable as a formatted PDF that can be attached to any matter in any system. Native integrations with Clio and Lawmatics are on the roadmap for post-beta. The PDF today is production-quality — it has the full timeline, source documents, and verified facts formatted for firm use.
❓ "How do we know the AI extracted the right facts?"
Every extracted fact shows which document it came from. Items the platform couldn't confirm are explicitly listed as "Requiring Confirmation." The source documents are always available for direct download — the platform supplements attorney review, it doesn't replace it.
❓ "What if workers don't upload enough documents?"
The platform handles partial records well. Even 3–4 documents produce a useful packet. When documents are missing, the platform flags them specifically (e.g., "timecards for the full employment period not uploaded") and has a built-in workflow to request them from the worker before or after the consultation.

The 5-Minute Demo Script

Use this structure when showing the platform. Adapt to who's in the room.

Before the demo

Pull up Marcus Rivera's intake — it's the sample intake with 12 documents covering the full employment arc: offer letter → paystubs → HR complaint → HR response → schedule change → written warning → meal break log → final paystub → termination letter → coworker statement.

  1. Open with the firm dashboard (30 sec). "This is what your team sees when a new intake arrives. Worker name, employer, document count, last documented event. Enough to decide if it's worth opening — before you touch a single document."
  2. Open the intake, show the timeline (90 sec). "Here's the reconstructed chronology. October 3rd — HR complaint filed. October 6th — HR response received, 3 days later. November 1st — schedule changed, 29 days after the complaint. December 14th — written warning. September 2024 — terminated, 11 months after the complaint. The platform built this from the documents. Your team didn't have to."
  3. Show Priority Questions (30 sec). "These are the 8 questions your associate would normally spend 30 minutes building. What did the worker report to HR? What did HR say back? What reason does the warning give? Does the termination letter match the warning? The packet hands this to whoever does the consultation."
  4. Show Evidence Support (30 sec). "For each claim type — HR complaint, employer response, schedule change, written warning, termination — we show whether a document exists. Not 'is this a good case.' Just: is the record there to support the allegation?"
  5. Show Items Requiring Confirmation (30 sec). "These are the things we can't confirm from documents alone. Start date discrepancy. Coworker statement dated post-termination. Termination reason not yet read. These are the exact questions you'd ask in the first 5 minutes of a consultation — now you walk in already knowing what to ask."
  6. Download the PDF (30 sec). "One click. This goes into your matter system, gets shared with the attorney doing the consultation, or gets attached to the file. No reformatting."
  7. Close (30 sec). "What you just saw took your team roughly 4 minutes to review instead of 45. That's the difference between screening 5 intakes a week and screening 20."
Section 11

What one3Seven Is NOT

This matters for ethics conversations and for positioning against competitors.

Not a Lead Generation Service

We don't promise volume. We don't buy leads or scrape directories. Workers find us or are sent to us by a firm. The value is in the quality and organization of each intake — not the number of names we deliver.

Not a Case Assessment Tool

one3Seven never scores cases, predicts outcomes, or tells a firm whether a case is worth taking. It organizes facts. Attorneys decide what those facts mean and whether to represent the worker.

Not a Law Firm

one3Seven does not provide legal advice. Workers submitting intake are not creating an attorney-client relationship with one3Seven. Every packet includes this disclaimer prominently.

Not a Practice Management System

one3Seven handles the pre-engagement intake phase only. It does not replace Clio, Lawmatics, or any matter management system. It plugs in before those tools via the PDF export.
