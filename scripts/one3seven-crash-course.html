01
The One-Sentence Truth

one3Seven organizes a worker's story and records before attorney review — so employment law firms begin with a structured chronology and source documents, not a pile of files and a phone call.

That's it. That's the whole product. Every feature, every screen, every design decision either serves that sentence or it shouldn't be there.

Three Things the Attorney Gets

1
A structured timeline
Documented events in chronological order, each linked to its source file. No floating claims.
2
Organized records
Every uploaded file categorized by type — payroll, HR, time records, termination — with nothing buried.
3
Clear gaps
What's missing is flagged before the first call, so the consultation starts on what matters.

Three Things the Attorney Does Not Get

  • A claim assessment, strength score, or case-value estimate
  • Legal conclusions about what happened or who is at fault
  • A filing-deadline guarantee or agency determination
The Governing Principle

"The platform organizes. The attorney reviews. The worker controls the sharing. No one on the platform makes legal decisions."

Why This Matters

Employment intake has a bottleneck — not access to attorneys, but disorganized information arriving before the first conversation. Workers have records scattered across phones, emails, and folders. Attorneys spend the first consultation building a timeline that could have existed before they arrived. one3Seven closes that gap.

It does not close the legal access gap. It does not replace legal advice. It removes one specific friction point in a specific part of the workflow.

02
Is / Is Not

Memorize the "Is Not" column. It's where the legal and product risk lives.

✓ one3Seven Is
  • A guided intake tool for employment records
  • A document upload and organization platform
  • A structured review interface for firms
  • A source-tracing system (events → files)
  • A chronology builder
  • A gap-detection and document-request tool
  • A firm-controlled workflow (review, request, accept, decline)
  • An intake routing platform (firm code or network)
✗ one3Seven Is Not
  • A law firm or legal services provider
  • A source of legal advice for any party
  • A claim-strength scorer or case evaluator
  • A filing-deadline calculator or deadline guarantor
  • A referral marketplace or firm matchmaker
  • A product supervised by a licensed attorney
  • A guarantee of review, response, or representation
  • A verified fact source — output requires attorney review
⚠ Non-Negotiable

These definitions cannot be softened, blurred, or footnoted away in any pitch, email, demo, press piece, or investor deck. If anyone on the team misrepresents them, correct it immediately.

What This Means in Practice

If someone asks…The answer is…
"Does it tell me if I have a case?"No. The attorney decides that after reviewing your organized intake.
"Does it know when my deadline is?"No. It surfaces relevant dates for timely attorney review. Deadlines depend on claim type and jurisdiction.
"Is it supervised by a lawyer?"No. Firm accounts are reviewed by the founder during beta. No licensed attorney supervises operations.
"Will the firm definitely respond?"No. Firms review routed intakes independently. No response is guaranteed.
"Can I use it to sue my employer?"one3Seven organizes your records for attorney review. An attorney decides whether to represent you.
03
The Two Users

👤 Workers

Employees who believe they've been wronged — wage theft, retaliation, wrongful termination, discrimination. Often anxious, unsure what they have, unsure if it's enough.

  • Need help telling their story clearly
  • Need to know which records matter
  • Need control over who sees their information
  • Need plain language throughout
  • Need transparency on where their intake stands

⚖️ Firms

Employment law attorneys and practices who evaluate potential clients. Their constraint: time. Every minute spent organizing documents before the consultation is a minute not spent on the matter itself.

  • Need faster initial review without losing accuracy
  • Need a chronology before the first consultation
  • Need direct access to source documents
  • Need clear signals about what's missing
  • Need fewer back-and-forth collection steps

✓ How to Think About It

Workers are not clients until represented in writing. Firms are not endorsements. The platform is the infrastructure between them — it never takes a side, never makes a recommendation, never guarantees anything for either party.

04
How the Product Works

Worker Flow

1
Arrives via firm link or direct sign-up
Firm code is captured and stored automatically.
2
Answers 8 guided questions
Employment context, key people, timeline, what happened. Stored as-told — not transformed.
3
Tags the matter type
Wage theft, overtime, retaliation, termination, discrimination, etc. Organizational tags only.
4
Uploads records
PDFs, docs. System categorizes, extracts text, and stores securely.
5
Intake is organized automatically
Timeline built, gaps flagged, structured summary assembled.
6
Routes to firm and responds to requests
Worker controls sharing. Uploads requested documents. Tracks status in plain language.

Firm Flow

1
Receives intake in review queue
Status: new, preview, full access, accepted, not pursuing.
2
Opens intake review
Worker context, organized chronology, source-linked intelligence, document categories, missing-record flags.
3
Reviews original files
Full-access route. Source documents are the final authority. Platform output is the starting point.
4
Requests documents if needed
Selects category + note. Worker receives and responds. Firm notified when docs arrive.
5
Makes intake decision
Accept, decline, or continue reviewing. All decisions are the attorney's. Platform records the outcome.
The Loop in One Line

Worker submits → platform organizes → firm reviews → attorney decides → outcome recorded. Every step is visible to the right party. Nothing is automated past the organization step.

05
The Mind Layer

The intelligence system is the most likely thing to be misrepresented. Read this carefully.

✓ The Mind Layer Does
  • Extract text from uploaded files (PDF text layer, TXT)
  • Categorize documents by type
  • Extract dates, employer names, key people
  • Build a dated, source-linked chronology
  • Surface relevant excerpts from documents
  • Calculate days between documented events
  • Flag missing or absent common record types
  • Extract structured facts per document category
  • Label all worker-provided content as worker-provided
  • Synthesize per-document facts into an intake-level view
✗ The Mind Layer Does Not
  • Determine whether a legal claim exists
  • Score claim strength or assign a grade
  • Decide who is telling the truth
  • Confirm that extracted facts are accurate
  • Determine whether retaliation occurred
  • Calculate a guaranteed filing deadline
  • Predict case outcomes
  • Replace original document review
  • Process scanned image files (no OCR)
  • Replace attorney judgment — ever

Two Processing Paths

Phase 2A — Deterministic

  • Runs on every intake automatically
  • Text extraction, filename-based classification
  • Rule-based timeline construction
  • Banned-output guardrails (no legal conclusions)
  • Always produces output — quality scales with records

Phase 2B — Intelligence

  • Runs when a full-access firm first opens intake
  • Claude API extracts structured facts per document
  • Synthesis produces timing cards, key quotes, facts
  • Confirmed operational as of June 2026
  • Non-blocking — failure never breaks base review
⚠ Output Quality Varies

A complete record set produces a rich intelligence panel. An intake with only a termination letter produces a sparse one. Both are correct behavior — the records are sparse, not the platform. Never represent demo-level output as guaranteed for all intakes.

08
Safety Rules

These are not guidelines. They are hard limits. The platform stays safe because these are non-negotiable.

Data and Privacy

  • No real worker data in production before security gates (RLS, org recovery, consent flow) are confirmed
  • No private worker information — narrative, allegations, document excerpts — in notification emails
  • No client-side API keys or secrets. Everything credentials-related is server-side only
  • No cross-account access. A firm sees only intakes routed to their firm ID
  • No preview-access firm viewing documents gated to full-access routes
  • Consent is recorded before any intake is shared with a firm — this is a legal record

Product Integrity

  • No AI output without source attribution where available
  • No demo feature represented as operational unless it works for real intakes
  • No legal conclusions, claim-strength language, or deadline guarantees in any user-facing copy
  • "Verified" never appears on information that hasn't been independently confirmed
  • No implication of an attorney-client relationship through any platform language
  • No silent failures that leave workers believing their information was saved when it wasn't

What Requires Founder Approval

  • Any public statement about legal compliance posture
  • Any claim about attorney involvement in operations
  • Any new firm onboarded to the beta
  • Any press, media, or public announcement
  • Any use of real worker data in a test, demo, or investor presentation
  • Any change to RLS policies or security settings
⚠ The Absolute Boundary

The platform organizes. It does not advise, conclude, guarantee, or represent. When any person, feature, or message crosses that line — stop and correct it before moving forward.

09
Engineering Rules

For developers, contractors, and anyone reviewing or approving code changes.

The Governing Rule

"One prompt, one defect, one expected behavior change. No opportunistic cleanup. No scope expansion during the pilot stabilization sprint."

Every Change Follows This Order

  1. Read first. Audit the relevant files before touching anything.
  2. Identify root cause with evidence, not assumption.
  3. Create a clean backup copy with a timestamp before any edit.
  4. Minimal patch. Change only what is required. Nothing else.
  5. Build. npm run build. Zero new errors required.
  6. Run targeted tests. Document any pre-existing failures.
  7. Smoke test the specific expected behavior change on production.
  8. Deploy. From the clean copy, via vercel.cmd --prod.
  9. Stop. Do not begin the next fix in the same session.

Do Not Touch — Ever Without Investigation

P1 Sprint — In Order

  1. RLS verification — read-only audit of all derived-data tables. No changes until matrix is complete.
  2. Pending-extraction smoke test — open intake d7123613, confirm auto-trigger fires once, confirm intelligence renders after reload.
  3. Organization recovery — detect and handle stale "Organizing Records" state. Must not duplicate data.
  4. Voice input — hide entry point if transcription cannot be proven. Do not delete underlying code.
  5. Firm email notifications — one channel, two events. Email failure must never block intake workflow.
  6. Event timing investigation — read-only first. Determine why timing cards don't render for all intakes.
