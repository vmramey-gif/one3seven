## The one-liner
**one3seven turns a worker's scattered records into one clear, dated, source-linked timeline — so a firm opens an organized file and decides in minutes, instead of building it from a shoebox.**

## The problem firms feel
Every new matter starts the same way: a worker shows up with a story and a pile — pay stubs, an HR complaint, a write-up, texts, a termination letter. Someone on staff burns the first hour (or week) just assembling it before an attorney can even look. That's slow, expensive, and it's where good cases stall in limbo.

## What one3seven does
- Takes the worker's account + their records.
- Builds a **dated timeline**, **categorizes the documents**, and **links every fact to the source document it came from.**
- Hands the firm a structured intake they open and decide on — **fast.**
- Surfaces what's missing ("additional information that may help") — never a verdict.

## The five objections (and the answers)

**1. "My clients don't want to talk to a robot."**
They don't have to. one3seven isn't a conversational bot — it organizes the records and story your client already has. That objection is exactly why we built it this way.

**2. "Isn't this practicing law / a UPL risk?"**
No. It **organizes and reflects, never concludes** — no legal advice, no strategy, no case evaluation. You evaluate everything. California's State Bar puts the duty on the deploying attorney and describes this exact pattern (private model, human-in-the-loop, source-traceable) as the compliant one; we just make it easy for you to discharge that duty.

**3. "What happens to my client's data?"**
Your data isn't used to train models; access is controlled; every fact traces to its source. (Full data-posture sheet available on request.)

**4. "How is this different from Clio/MyCase we already use?"**
Those manage your practice after intake. one3seven organizes the **incoming** mess — the client's records into a source-linked timeline — so what lands in your system is already clean. It sits in front of your tools, not against them.

**5. "Can I trust the AI? Doesn't it hallucinate?"**
Every fact in the packet **links to its source document** — click and verify the original. Nothing is asserted without a traceable origin. (And the source-linked design is how Anthropic itself positions trustworthy deployments.)
