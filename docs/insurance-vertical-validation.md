# Insurance Vertical — Validation Playbook (Idea Vault)

**Status:** Validation only. NOT a build order. Evening discovery calls, zero engineering hours, CELA sprint untouched until 3 paying CA employment firms.

**Core rule (same DNA as one3seven v1):** organize the claim, preserve the client's story, source-link every fact, surface missing documents — and **never** provide coverage opinions, claim values, settlement advice, legal advice, or insurer-payment conclusions.

---

## Who it's for

Best suited for people who work directly with **claim documentation**: intake, timelines, photos, receipts, estimates, inventories, correspondence, supporting records. The product must stay limited to organizing information, preserving the policyholder's own words, identifying missing materials, and source-linking records. It must **not** give coverage opinions, claim valuations, settlement recommendations, legal advice, or tell anyone whether a claim should be paid.

**Primary target — Public adjusters.** In California, a public insurance adjuster acts, for compensation, on behalf of the *insured* to negotiate/effect the settlement of a property claim (and assists in the preparation, presentation, and settlement of a claim). Closest fit because they work for the insured, not the carrier, and their work depends on documentation, timelines, estimates, inventories, photos, and claim correspondence.

**Second target — Independent P&C agents/brokers who stay involved after a loss.** Not the ones who just sell policies and hand claims to the carrier — the ones who help clients gather materials, locate policy docs, organize photos, collect receipts, track claim numbers, keep correspondence together. Positioned as a **claim-documentation organization tool, not an adjusting tool.**

**Later target — Disability / life / long-term-care.** Also documentation-heavy, but a different workflow from property. Not the first lane.

**Weak / skip first:** captive agents (tied to one carrier's systems), carrier-side adjusters (they're the counterparty, not the buyer), pure new-policy sales agents (no claims = no documentation pain).

**Cleanest first lane:** property claim documentation — public adjusters + independent property-focused agents/brokers who actually help clients organize claim materials.

**One-line qualifier:** "Do you help clients organize claim materials after a loss, or do you mostly sell policies and hand claims off to the carrier?" Help organize → real prospect. Hand off completely → thank them and move on.

## The 11 discovery questions

1. **"Walk me through what happens from the moment a client contacts you about a loss to the point where the claim materials are submitted or reviewed."** — Their real workflow, unled. Listen for who collects photos, asks for receipts, builds the timeline, reviews estimates, talks to the carrier, and where info gets lost.
2. **"How much of that process is spent chasing the client for documents, photos, receipts, timelines, or missing details?"** — Is the pain real. Lots of chasing → clear reason to exist.
3. **"What do clients most often forget, lose, or organize poorly when documenting a claim?"** — These answers become your intake prompts (photos before cleanup, receipts, proof of ownership, repair invoices, temp-housing costs, damaged contents, carrier emails, adjuster letters, police/fire/plumber reports, mitigation invoices, exact dates).
4. **"When a claim is delayed, disputed, or requires more follow-up, what is usually missing or unclear?"** — Safer than "underpaid/lowballed." Keeps it on documentation, not conclusions.
5. **"How many active claims or claim-related client issues are you usually touching in a busy month?"** — Volume = whether it supports a real tool.
6. **"What is the first thing you wish every policyholder had done before calling you?"** — Reveals the exact early behavior the product should prompt.
7. **"Where do you have to be careful in your role — what can you help organize, and what would cross into adjusting, coverage advice, settlement advice, or legal advice?"** — Critical CA compliance boundary. Safe lane: organize, summarize, date, categorize, source-link, identify gaps. Risky lane: claim value, coverage opinion, settlement position, what the carrier owes, whether a denial is wrong, negotiating.
8. **"Have you seen E&O issues, complaints, or compliance problems arise from claim-related communications? What usually triggered them?"** — What language/features to avoid (promised coverage/payment, advised not to accept, failed to document a request, delayed reporting, advice outside role).
9. **"If a client came to you with their timeline, photos, receipts, estimates, carrier letters, and other records already organized and source-linked before your first review, what would that be worth in time saved or cleaner review?"** — Value without promising better results. Use: time saved, cleaner documentation, fewer follow-ups, easier review, clearer record. Avoid: "better outcomes," "fewer denials."
10. **"Would a tool like that be something your office would pay for, something the client would pay for, or something that only makes sense if included in your process?"** — Identifies the payer.
11. **"If this existed and worked, who would decide whether your office could use it — you, the owner, compliance, operations, or someone else?"** — How a real sale happens.

**Soft close:** "If a tool organized a client's claim materials into a clean, source-linked packet before your first review — without giving coverage opinions, claim values, settlement recommendations, or legal advice — would you want to be one of the first people to test it?"

**Log after each call:** their workflow · the #1 thing clients miss · the E&O line they named · their answer to Q9 (value) · the payer (Q10).

## Positioning & compliance (verbatim — do not soften)

**Describe it as:** a policyholder-side claim documentation organizer. **A source-linked claim documentation packet before the first professional review.**

**Never describe it as:** a tool that adjusts, values, or negotiates claims, determines coverage, tells clients what they're owed, or helps them fight the carrier.

**Disclaimer:** "This packet organizes information provided by the policyholder and uploaded records. It does not determine coverage, claim value, liability, settlement amount, insurer obligations, or whether any claim should be paid. A licensed professional must review the materials and make any claim, coverage, valuation, or settlement determinations."

> ⚠️ If this ever moves from validation to build: the CA public-adjuster definition, the E&O boundary, and this disclaimer need a real insurance-law review — exactly like UPL got for one3seven v1. Do not ship the insurance version on a self-drafted compliance posture.
